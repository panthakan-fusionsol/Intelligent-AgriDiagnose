CLASSIFICATION CRITERIA (Visual Decision Rules)

1. healthy
   - No visible disease symptoms
   - Clean green leaves without lesions, spots, pustules, or abnormal discoloration
   - Natural color variation is acceptable

2. rust
   - **Distinctive feature**: Raised, powdery, reddish-brown to orange pustules (bumps)
   - Pustules contain spores and appear in clusters
   - Often widespread on leaf surface
   - **If you see raised pustules → classify as rust immediately**

3. northern corn leaf blight
   - **Distinctive feature**: Long, elongated brown lesions (typically >3cm)
   - Cigar-shaped or elliptical lesions
   - Lesions have tapered or pointed ends
   - May merge into large necrotic areas
   - Different from "leaf sheath and leaf spot" due to LENGTH and TAPERED SHAPE

4. downy mildew
   - **Distinctive feature**: Longitudinal striping pattern
   - Alternating pale yellow/chlorotic bands with dark green bands
   - Stripes run parallel to leaf veins (lengthwise)
   - Clear, organized separation between colored zones
   - Severe cases: entire leaf pale yellow but retains banded structure
   - Different from "virus" due to PARALLEL ORGANIZED BANDS

5. virus
   - **Distinctive feature**: Mosaic/mottled pattern
   - Irregular yellow-pale patches scattered randomly
   - Chlorotic spots interspersed with normal green areas
   - NO parallel bands or linear organization
   - Gives "patchwork quilt" appearance
   - Different from "downy mildew" due to RANDOM, IRREGULAR distribution

6. leaf sheath and leaf spot
   - Brown lesions with irregular, variable shapes and sizes
   - Affects both leaf sheath (กาบ) and leaf blade (ใบ)
   - NOT elongated/cigar-shaped (that would be blight)
   - NOT raised pustules (that would be rust)
   - May be circular, oval, or irregular
   - Can have necrotic centers or halos
   - Use when lesions don't clearly match rust or blight characteristics

DECISION PRIORITY (Apply in this order)

Priority 1: Check for RUST pustules
   → Raised, powdery, reddish-brown bumps? → "rust"

Priority 2: Check LESION MORPHOLOGY
   → Long (>3cm), tapered, cigar-shaped? → "northern corn leaf blight"
   → Short, irregular brown spots? → "leaf sheath and leaf spot"

Priority 3: Check COLOR PATTERNS
   → Parallel yellow/green stripes (lengthwise)? → "downy mildew"
   → Random yellow mosaic/mottling? → "virus"

Priority 4: No symptoms visible?
   → "healthy"

KEY DISAMBIGUATION RULES

When confused between classes:

**rust vs leaf sheath and leaf spot**
- rust = RAISED pustules (3D texture, bumpy)
- leaf sheath and leaf spot = FLAT lesions (2D, no elevation)

**northern corn leaf blight vs leaf sheath and leaf spot**
- northern corn leaf blight = LONG lesions (>3cm), TAPERED ends, cigar-shaped
- leaf sheath and leaf spot = SHORTER lesions, IRREGULAR shape, no consistent tapering

**downy mildew vs virus**
- downy mildew = PARALLEL STRIPES running lengthwise (organized)
- virus = RANDOM PATCHES of yellow/green (chaotic mosaic)

**Multiple symptoms present**
- Choose the MOST DOMINANT and EXTENSIVE symptom
- If rust pustules are anywhere visible → always "rust"
- If torn between two others → choose the one covering more leaf area
